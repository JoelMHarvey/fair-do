# Faresay Marketing Kit

The vision is the message. Everything below leads with it, not with features or price.

## The vision (the crux of everything)
> We're technologists with a heart. We believe therapists should earn enough to focus on the work, that therapy should reach more people, and that no company should profit from human suffering.

Three pillars, repeat them everywhere:
1. **Fair pay** — therapists keep the great majority (currently 90% for founders).
2. **Access** — lower, transparent pricing so more people can afford help.
3. **No profiteering** — a small fixed fee to keep the lights on, nothing more.

Tone: warm, plain-spoken, hopeful. Never clinical, never salesy, never fear-based. We don't bash competitors — we just quietly do it differently.

---

## Messaging hierarchy (use in this order)
1. **Lead with belief**, not price. "We think no one should profit from human suffering."
2. **Then the human benefit.** Therapists who can focus; clients who can afford to start.
3. **Then the proof.** BACP-verified, encrypted video, transparent fee.
4. **CTA last.** Find a therapist / Join as a founding therapist.

## Magazine / blog article (draft — ~700 words, ready to adapt)

### Title: *The hidden maths of online therapy — and why we rebuilt it*

There's a number most online therapy platforms would rather you didn't see. On some, for every pound a client pays, the therapist keeps barely a third. The rest goes to the platform.

Sit with that. The person who trained for years, who holds space for someone's worst week, who carries the quiet weight of the work home — keeps a third. Meanwhile the client, often reaching out at a hard moment, pays a premium. And a company in the middle profits from both sides of someone's pain.

We're technologists. We've built plenty of things that optimise for growth and margin. But when we looked closely at this one, the maths didn't sit right with us. Mental health isn't a market inefficiency to exploit. It's people.

So we built Faresay on a different premise. Take a small, fixed, transparent fee — enough to run secure video, payments, and support — and pass the rest to the therapist. For our founding therapists, that's 90%. No subscription traps for clients. No tactics to keep people booking when they're ready to stop.

The surprising part? Fairer to therapists turns out to be cheaper for clients, too. When you're not funding a 65% platform cut, the price comes down on its own. Fair pay and wider access aren't in tension — the markup was the problem all along.

None of this means cutting corners on safety. Every therapist on Faresay holds current registration with BACP, UKCP, BPS or NCS — verified before they take a single booking, with an audit trail, and re-checked annually. Sessions run over encrypted, GDPR-compliant video. We store only what we must, and we will never sell what people tell us.

We're early, and we're honest about that. We don't have thousands of therapists yet. What we have is a clear belief: that no company should profit from human suffering, that therapists should be paid well enough to focus on the work, and that help should reach more of the people who need it. If that resonates — whether you're looking for support, or you're a therapist tired of giving away two-thirds of your fee — we'd love for you to be one of the people who builds this with us.

Therapy that works. Priced fairly. That's the whole idea.

*— The Faresay team. faresay.com*

## Short-form video scripts (YT Shorts / TikTok / Reels — 20–40s each)

### Script 1 — "The maths" (founder to camera)
- **Hook (0–3s):** "On some therapy apps, the therapist keeps about a third of what you pay. Here's where the rest goes."
- **Middle (3–25s):** Plain talk: "We're technologists. We build for margin all the time. But profiting off someone's worst week? No. So we flipped it — therapists keep 90%, you pay less, we take a small fixed fee. That's it."
- **CTA (25–30s):** "Therapy that works, priced fairly. Link in bio."
- *Visual:* simple text-on-screen of the split (pie that flips), warm tone, no stock-sad imagery.

### Script 2 — "For therapists" (direct)
- **Hook:** "Therapists: how much of your online session fee do you actually keep?"
- **Middle:** "On the big platforms, often around a third. On Faresay, founding therapists keep 90%. Set your own rates, your own hours. We bring the clients and handle the admin."
- **CTA:** "Keep what you earn. faresay.com/for-therapists."

### Script 3 — "Permission" (gentle, client-facing)
- **Hook:** "You don't have to be in crisis to talk to someone."
- **Middle:** "Therapy shouldn't be a luxury. We made it more affordable by paying therapists fairly instead of taking a huge cut. Verified, UK-registered therapists. Secure video. No subscription."
- **CTA:** "When you're ready — faresay.com."

### Script 4 — "Why we built it" (mission)
- **Hook:** "We think no company should profit from human suffering."
- **Middle:** "That belief is the whole reason Faresay exists. Fair pay for therapists. Real access for people who need help. A small, honest fee — nothing more."
- **CTA:** "Built by technologists with a heart. faresay.com/about."

**Production notes:** founder-to-camera beats polished ads for trust. Captions always (sound-off viewing). One idea per video. Never use distressing imagery. End every video on the vision, not the discount. Post 3–5×/week, reuse the best as paid once organic proves a hook.

## Contact lists — how to build them (not fabricated)
I won't invent contacts. Build real lists from:
- **Therapists:** BACP "Find a therapist" directory, UKCP register, Counselling Directory, Psychology Today UK — filter by online/UK, export name + listed email/site. LinkedIn Sales Navigator search "BACP registered".
- **Press:** each magazine's masthead / "pitch us" page (Happiful, Psychologies, Welldoing, Metro, Stylist). *Therapy Today* for the therapist-pay story.
- **Communities:** UK counsellor Facebook groups, therapist Slack/Discord, relevant subreddits (read rules; value-first).
Keep a simple CRM sheet: name · type (therapist/press/community) · contact · channel · status · last touch.

## Launch sequence (first 6 weeks)
1. **Weeks 1–2:** therapist outreach (you have the most leverage here — supply first). Goal: 5–10 founding therapists live.
2. **Week 2:** publish the article on the Faresay blog + pitch to 1–2 magazines.
3. **Weeks 3–4:** start short-form video (scripts above), 3–5/week. Drive to faresay.com.
4. **Weeks 3–6:** soft client outreach to warm network + referral loop (£10 each way) does the compounding.
5. **Ongoing:** every new therapist gets the £25 peer-referral nudge — supply grows itself.

Measure: therapists live, first bookings, cost-per-signup (once paid starts), referral activations.
