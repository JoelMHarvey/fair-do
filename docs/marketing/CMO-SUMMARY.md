# fair-do Marketing Plan — CMO Summary

## Executive Summary

fair-do enters a £6–8 billion UK tutoring market with a structurally superior economic model: zero commission on tutor earnings, zero service fee at the parent checkout, and a transparent flat subscription (Free / Pro at £29/month / School at £79/month). The business is built on a single insight — that incumbent platforms extract 25–40% from every lesson indefinitely, misaligning their incentives with both tutor success and parent trust. fair-do's model flips that: we earn when tutors stay, not when tutors teach.

The marketing strategy across six specialists coalesces into three parallel tracks running simultaneously from launch.

**Supply-side acquisition** (tutors) is the critical path. Without sufficient tutor supply, no parent channel can convert. The founding tutor programme — targeting 200 signups and 30 Founding Member enrolments by Week 6 — is the immediate priority. Six tutor emails, a social calendar anchored to the commission maths argument, SEO targeting "alternatives to Tutorful" and "online tutoring platform no commission," paid search on competitor-displacement keywords, and outreach to the Tutors' Association and PGCE providers all work in parallel toward this goal. Tutor CAC target is under £18 blended, with a Phase 1 paid budget ceiling of £1,500.

**Demand-side activation** (parents) follows supply. Parent-facing content — five portal emails, parent-focused social content from Week 4, and SEO targeting "how much does a private tutor cost UK" — begins immediately but paid parent acquisition does not scale until Month 2, once tutor supply in at least eight subjects is sufficient to convert search demand.

**Brand and authority building** underpins both tracks: 12 SEO articles across six months, a disciplined backlink strategy targeting TES, Guardian Education, Which?, and Mumsnet, a visual identity system built around warm professionalism, and a PR playbook with embargoed launch data for education press.

The north star metric is weekly active tutoring hours on platform. Phase 1 gate: 150 hours/week by Week 6. Everything in this plan either directly drives tutor sessions or builds the brand infrastructure that sustains them.

## Inconsistencies / Flags

**1. Tutor CAC target discrepancy — Metrics vs Paid Media**

The Metrics Framework sets a blended tutor CAC target of **< £18** at Phase 2 (Month 6). The Paid Media Plan sets a blended paid CAC target to a free signup of **£35–£55**, with a blended paid CAC to a converting Pro customer of **£80–£120** in Months 1–3 declining to **£80–£100** by Month 6. These are not necessarily contradictory — the Metrics Framework's £18 is a blended all-channel CAC (including organic, referral, and social at near-zero marginal cost), while the Paid Media figure is paid-channel only. However, the Metrics Framework does not make this distinction explicit. If any reader interprets the £18 figure as a paid-channel CAC ceiling, the paid media plan is irreconcilable with it. **Resolution needed:** The Metrics Framework should clarify that the £18 blended CAC assumes significant organic and referral volume offsetting higher paid CAC, and the Paid Media Plan should state that paid-only CAC sits above the blended target by design.

**2. Parent CAC target discrepancy — Metrics vs Paid Media**

The Metrics Framework sets parent blended CAC at **< £12**. The Paid Media Plan sets a practical paid parent CAC ceiling of **£10–£15 in Months 1–4**, rising to **£15–£25 in Months 5–6**, with a stated flexible ceiling of £25. The Paid Media Plan's Month 5–6 upper bound (£25) and the Metrics Framework's ceiling (£12) are inconsistent even accounting for a paid/blended distinction. The Paid Media Plan also frames parent CAC in indirect LTV terms (each parent extends tutor tenure, adding £14–£58 to tutor LTV), which the Metrics Framework does not reference at all. **Resolution needed:** Agree a single parent CAC ceiling that both frameworks reference, with explicit notes on whether it is paid-only, blended, or indirect-LTV-adjusted.

**3. MRR timing discrepancy — Metrics Framework vs Press Release**

The Metrics Framework financial model shows MRR of **£145 in Month 1** and **£435 in Month 2**. The Email sequence (Email 1, tutor welcome) and the Metrics Phase 1 table both describe a target of **£435 MRR at end of Week 6** (which is approximately Month 1.5). These are close but not identical: the financial model implies £435 arrives in Month 2, the Phase 1 target implies it arrives by Week 6 (mid-Month 2 at latest). This is unlikely to cause operational confusion but should be aligned to avoid conflicting board/investor reporting.

**4. Founding tutor programme close date — Social Calendar vs PR Playbook**

The Social Calendar (Week 6 posts) states the founding tutor programme/rate "closes this month" and "closes at the end of this month," implying a hard deadline in Month 1–2. The PR Playbook and Email sequence make no mention of a closing date and frame founding tutor access as ongoing. If the social posts create genuine scarcity pressure (intended), the email sequence and PR materials need to mirror this urgency or tutors who receive the email without the social post will have no equivalent prompt. **Resolution needed:** Either set and communicate a firm founding tutor deadline across all channels, or remove the closing date language from social content to avoid a false urgency signal that is not backed by policy.

**5. AI feature attribution — Social Calendar vs Asset Brief**

The Week 5 LinkedIn post (Behind the Build thread) lists the tech stack: "Excalidraw, Claude (Anthropic)." The Visual Asset Brief and Email sequence reference the AI post-session summaries feature without naming the underlying model. The SEO Post 8 ("How AI Is Changing the Way Tutors Plan Lessons") is deliberately non-specific about the AI provider. There is no inconsistency per se, but there is no agreed policy on whether fair-do names Claude/Anthropic in public-facing content. Given Anthropic's brand has value to tutors as a trust signal, this warrants a deliberate decision rather than inconsistent practice across channels.

**6. Domain referenced inconsistently**

The Metrics Framework, SEO Plan, and Paid Media Plan all reference **fair-do.com**. The press release, social content, email footers, and PR Playbook all reference **fair-do.co.uk**. This needs to be resolved before any paid media or press goes live — a mismatch between the advertised URL and the landing domain will waste paid spend, break tracking, and undermine credibility in press coverage.

**7. VAT status inconsistency**

Email 6 (Pro upgrade ask) includes a footer note: "VAT not included — fair-do is not yet VAT registered." The press release and all other materials make no mention of VAT status. If fair-do is not VAT-registered, this should be noted wherever Pro pricing (£29/month) is stated in financial projections and media materials — the MRR model should clarify whether figures are inclusive or exclusive of future VAT liability. The current omission creates a potential mismatch between stated MRR and actual revenue once the VAT threshold is approached.

## 30-Day Action List

**1. Resolve domain (fair-do.com vs fair-do.co.uk) — Day 1**
Before any paid spend, press release distribution, or email deployment. Update every asset, tracking URL, sitemap, and canonical tag to the single confirmed domain. This is the highest-risk inconsistency in the plan.

**2. Install and verify conversion tracking — Days 1–3**
Meta Pixel, GA4 with named events (ProfileComplete, SessionDelivered, ProSubscription, ParentEnquiry), and UTM naming convention must be live and verified in debug tools before any paid campaign launches. No spend goes live without confirmed event firing.

**3. Agree and document CAC definitions across teams — Days 1–5**
The Metrics, Paid Media, and Finance teams must align on a single definition of tutor CAC and parent CAC — paid-only, blended, and indirect-LTV-adjusted variants each documented separately. Circulate as a one-page reference before the first weekly dashboard is run.

**4. Launch tutor email welcome sequence — Days 1–7**
All six emails are written. The sequence should go live on Day 1 for any tutor who signs up. Test the full sequence in a staging environment, confirm send times, unsubscribe links, and mobile rendering before the first founding tutor receives Email 1.

**5. Publish technical SEO foundations — Days 1–7**
Implement the full 15-point technical SEO checklist: title tags, meta descriptions, OG tags, canonical tags, XML sitemap, robots.txt, Core Web Vitals audit, structured data (EducationalOrganization schema on homepage). Submit sitemap to Google Search Console and Bing Webmaster Tools. This work compounds from Day 1 — every week of delay is a week of lost indexing.

**6. Launch Google Search tutor campaigns — Days 3–7**
Campaign 1 (tutor acquisition) goes live in Week 1 with the five exact-match tutor keywords, manual CPC bidding, and negative keyword list applied. Budget: £700 in Month 1. Do not launch parent campaign (Campaign 2) until Week 3 when baseline tutor campaign data is available.

**7. Deploy founding tutor social content (Weeks 1–2 of calendar) — Days 1–14**
Post 1.1 (LinkedIn founder long-form), 1.2 (Instagram Reel), 1.3 (X thread), and 1.4 (TikTok) go live on launch day. Week 2 maths carousel content scheduled and ready. Confirm social handles are secured on all four platforms before any post references them.

**8. Send embargoed press release to TES, Guardian Education, and Schools Week — Days 1–3**
Use Template 1 (education press) with the earnings-gap data as the exclusive hook. Offer Rachel Okafor (TES) and Claudia Herring (Guardian) a founder interview and founding tutor case study. Target publication in Week 2 to coincide with social momentum.

**9. Publish SEO Posts 1 and 2 — Days 7–14**
"Why Tutorful's 0% Commission Isn't What It Sounds Like" and "How to Find Your First 5 Private Tutoring Students" are the highest-priority organic content pieces. Both target commercial-intent keywords with low-to-medium difficulty. Each needs internal links to the pricing page and signup page before publication.

**10. Commission and brief design team on Priority 1 visual assets — Days 1–7**
The logo suite (three lockup variants, icon-only, all formats), social profile images, and OG image must be complete before any social accounts go live or press release is distributed. Brief the designer using the Visual Asset Brief. Week 1 delivery target: logo SVG masters, social profile images for all five platforms, OG image. Without these, every piece of content published in the first 30 days is visually unbranded.
