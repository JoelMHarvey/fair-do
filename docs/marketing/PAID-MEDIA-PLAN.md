# fair-do Paid Media Plan — Months 1–6

---

## Executive Summary

Fair-do enters a £6–8 billion UK tutoring market with a structurally superior economic model and a product that already outbuilds the incumbents. The paid media strategy has two parallel jobs: acquire tutors first (they populate the supply side and their word-of-mouth converts parents), then accelerate parent acquisition once supply density justifies it. Months 1–3 are tutor-led and proof-gathering. Months 4–6 open the parent channel at scale as case studies, search impression share, and retargeting pools mature.

### Audience Targeting

**In-market audiences (layered onto search for bid adjustment):**
- "Online Education" (Google in-market)
- "Family Services" > "Tutoring and Test Preparation"
- Custom intent: people who searched for Tutorful, MyTutor, Spires, Superprof in the past 30 days (competitor URL list + branded terms list)

**Bid adjustments:**
- +25% for mobile on parent campaigns (parents book on phone)
- +20% for desktop on tutor campaigns (tutors research and sign up on laptop)
- +15% for 19:00–22:00 daily window (tutor browsing time after their evening lessons)
- +30% September, January, and May–June (exam season and re-enrolment peaks)
- Geographic: England, Wales, Scotland — bid down -20% Northern Ireland (lower density)

---

### Bid Strategy and Target CPA

**Months 1–2:** Manual CPC with Enhanced CPC enabled. Reason: conversion volume is too low for Smart Bidding to calibrate. Set manual bids at £1.80–£2.40 for tutor terms, £1.20–£1.80 for parent terms.

**Month 3 onwards:** Switch to Target CPA once 30+ conversions per campaign per month are recorded.

**Target CPA by campaign:**
- Tutor Acquisition (registration + profile completion): £18–£25
- Parent Acquisition (enquiry or first booking): £8–£14
- Brand Defence: uncapped — just maintain impression share above 85%

**Conversion actions to track (in priority order):**
1. Tutor profile completion (primary — indicates genuine intent)
2. Tutor free trial signup (secondary)
3. Parent enquiry sent or lesson booked (primary for parent campaigns)
4. Pricing page visit with 60+ seconds dwell (micro-conversion, used for retargeting pool only)

### 3 Audience Definitions

**Audience 1 — Custom Audience: Warm Tutor Signals**

Source: Upload email list of tutors who engaged with the launch sequence, joined the waitlist, or interacted with the organic social content. Supplement with website visitors to /tutors, /pricing, /pro pages (pixel-based, 30-day window).

Refinements: UK only. Age 24–55. Exclude anyone who has already completed registration (suppress via pixel event "CompleteRegistration"). Size target: 2,000–8,000 — small enough to be meaningful, large enough for delivery.

Use: Conversion campaign. Show the pricing detail ad (Variation 2 below). This audience already knows fair-do; the ad just needs to close.

---

**Audience 2 — Lookalike Audience: 2% Lookalike from Converted Tutors**

Source: Seed audience = tutors who completed profile setup (export from CRM once you have 100+ data points, typically by end of month 2). Build 2% Lookalike of UK population from this seed.

Refinements: Narrow by interests — "Teaching", "Private tutoring", "Education", "Self-employment UK". Layer Behaviours: "Small business owners", "Freelancers". Exclude existing website visitors (already in Custom Audience 1).

Use: Prospecting at scale from month 3. This is the highest-efficiency prospecting audience once the seed quality is established.

---

**Audience 3 — Interest-Based: Tutor Cold Prospecting**

Demographics: UK, Age 22–52, All genders (slight overindex female — tutoring is 60%+ female workforce in UK).

Interests (layered with OR logic): Teaching, Private tutoring, Online tutoring, Classroom management, TES (Times Educational Supplement), Education Technology, Teachwire. Add occupational titles where available: Teacher, Tutor, Teaching assistant, Private tutor.

Behaviours: Engaged shoppers (higher purchase intent), people who access Facebook/Instagram via education-adjacent content.

Exclude: Anyone with "Student" interest or age 22–24 without teaching interests (reduces wastage on current students).

Use: Cold prospecting months 1–3. Expect CPM of £6–£10, CTR of 0.8–1.5%, and CPA of £30–£45 before optimisation. Shift budget to Lookalike (Audience 2) once it's mature.

## TikTok Ads — Tutor Acquisition

### Who to Target

**Demographics:** UK, Age 23–38. The 35–52 tutor cohort is underrepresented on TikTok — focus on NQTs, recently qualified tutors, and the younger end of the experienced-tutor segment who are most active on the platform and most receptive to a disruptive financial argument.

**Interest targeting:** Education, Teaching, Career development, Self-employment, Side hustle, Personal finance.

**Behavioural:** Engaged with content about freelancing, teacher wellbeing, pay, working from home.

**Creator targeting (for Spark Ads):** Identify 3–5 UK teacher / tutor creators on TikTok who already post about tutoring, lesson prep, or teacher finances. Prioritise creators with 5,000–50,000 followers (higher engagement, lower cost, more authentic to audience than macro-influencers).

---

### Creative Brief: Spark Ad

**Concept: "I did the maths on my tutoring platform" (Organic-first, then boosted as Spark Ad)**

This should be created as an organic post first — ideally by the founder or a founding tutor, filmed as a genuine "I discovered something" video. TikTok's algorithm and audience reward authenticity over production value; a polished ad creative will underperform a genuine personal finance reveal.

**Script outline (30–35 seconds):**

Opening hook (0–3 sec): Direct to camera. "I've been tutoring for 3 years and I've just realised I've given £22,000 to my platform."

Development (3–20 sec): Hold up phone showing a simple spreadsheet or calculator. "I charge £40 per session. The platform takes 25%. That's £10 a session. I do 20 sessions a week. That's £200 a week, £800 a month, £9,600 a year — and I've been doing this for over two years. Over two years, that's £22,000 I've handed over for... what exactly? An account I manage myself, students I found myself, lessons I run myself."

Pivot (20–28 sec): "There's a platform called fair-do that takes literally nothing from your sessions. You pay £29 a month flat. That's it. For unlimited students, a video classroom, a whiteboard, everything. I switched last month and I cannot believe I waited this long."

CTA (28–35 sec): "Link in bio — free for up to 8 students, no card needed. Do your own maths." Text overlay: fair-do.co.uk

**Production notes:** Film vertical on iPhone. Natural lighting, home or tutoring background. Real calculator or spreadsheet is essential — the specific number is the hook. No polish. One take feel, with jump cuts if needed.

**Boost strategy:** Once the organic post has 48–72 hours of organic performance data, run it as a Spark Ad targeting the audience definition above. Do not boost before organic data — let the algorithm show what demographic is engaging, then amplify that.

**Segment 4 — Parent / student inquiry abandoned**

### Retargeting Frequency Caps

Apply across all retargeting: maximum 3 impressions per person per day, 15 impressions per person per week on Meta. On Google Display: maximum 5 impressions per day. High-frequency retargeting damages brand sentiment with a warmth-forward brand like fair-do.

## CAC Targets

### LTV Calculations

**Tutor LTV:**
- Average tutor stays on free plan for 1 month, then upgrades to Pro if they have more than 8 active students
- Expected Pro subscription tenure: 18 months (conservative — tutoring is a recurring practice, not a seasonal purchase)
- Pro plan monthly: £29
- Tutor LTV (Pro): £29 × 18 = £522
- Target tutor CAC: 30% of LTV = **£156.60, round to £155**
- In practice, target £35–£55 for free signups and accept that 30–40% will convert to Pro. Blended paid CAC to a paying Pro customer should sit below £120 in months 1–3, declining to £80–£100 by month 6 as optimisation compounds.

**Parent LTV (booking platform model):**
- Fair-do charges tutors, not parents — parents are not direct revenue. However, parent LTV matters because:
  1. More parents = more demand signal = tutor retention improves = tutor LTV extends
  2. Parent NPS drives word-of-mouth which reduces future paid CAC
- Model parent value as indirect: each active parent relationship extends tutor tenure by an estimated 0.5 months, adding approximately £14.50 to tutor LTV per parent acquired
- Practical parent CAC ceiling: **£12–£18**. Do not spend more than this per parent enquiry in months 1–4.
- If average parent books a tutor who then stays an additional 2 months on Pro = £58 additional tutor LTV. Parent CAC ceiling can flex to £25 in months 5–6 as this relationship is better understood.

**School / B2B LTV:**
- School plan: £79/month
- Expected tenure: 24 months (institutional contracts renew annually)
- LTV: £79 × 24 = £1,896
- B2B CAC ceiling: 30% = **£569**. Not addressed in this paid media plan — B2B acquisition is outbound sales and LinkedIn, not paid search/social. Track separately.

## Reporting Cadence

### Weekly Review (every Monday, 30 minutes)

Owner: Paid Media Lead

**Metrics to pull:**

Google Ads:
- Impressions, clicks, CTR by campaign
- CPC by ad group — flag any group where CPC has moved more than 20% week-on-week
- Conversions and CPA by campaign
- Quality Score changes (if Google Ads interface shows movement)
- Search terms report — add new negative keywords weekly in months 1–2 to tighten targeting

Meta:
- Reach, CPM, CTR (link click through rate) by audience
- Cost per result (registration or enquiry) by ad set
- Frequency — if any ad set is above 3.5 frequency, refresh creative or expand audience
- Video view rate (% watching to 3 seconds, % watching to 15 seconds) for video creatives

TikTok:
- Video views, average watch time
- Profile visits from ad
- Link clicks and CTR

**Weekly action threshold:** If CPA for any campaign exceeds 2× target CPA for 7 consecutive days, pause and investigate before spending further.

---

### Monthly Review (first Thursday of each month, 90 minutes)

Owner: Paid Media Lead + Founder

**Metrics to review:**

Performance:
- Total spend vs budget by channel
- Total conversions by type (tutor free signup, tutor Pro conversion, parent enquiry, parent booking)
- Blended CAC actual vs target by audience
- MRR growth and attribution — which paid channel drove the highest-LTV customers?
- Cohort analysis: of tutors acquired in month N, what percentage converted to Pro, and in what timeframe?

Creative performance:
- Which ad creative drove the lowest CPA? Which the highest?
- Which Meta audience (Custom, Lookalike, Interest) is most efficient?
- Are any Google ad copy variations consistently underperforming in ad strength or CTR? Pause and replace.

Budget decisions:
- Is Meta Parent spend producing a measurable impact on parent enquiry volume? If not, hold spend there and reallocate to tutor side.
- Is TikTok producing trackable signups? If UTM tracking shows zero registrations from TikTok in month 2, reduce spend and investigate attribution gap before month 3.
- Decision: carry reserve forward or deploy it against a seasonal spike?

Reporting output: A single-page performance summary (channel / spend / conversions / CPA / vs target / action). Share with founder by EOD on review day.

---

### Quarterly Review (end of month 3 and month 6)

**Deliverables:**
- Full funnel analysis: impression to click to registration to Pro conversion by channel
- Attribution model review — are last-click conversions misattributing? Run a position-based or data-driven attribution comparison
- Audience refresh: rebuild Lookalike seeds from latest Pro customer list; retire underperforming interest sets
- Creative refresh plan: all creatives running for 60+ days should be rotated or retested — creative fatigue is the primary cause of declining Meta performance
- CAC trend: is paid CAC improving month-on-month? Target 10–15% efficiency improvement per quarter through optimisation
- Budget recommendation for next quarter: present three scenarios (maintain, increase 20%, increase 40%) with projected conversion volumes at each

## Implementation Priorities — First 30 Days

1. Install Meta Pixel and Google Analytics 4 with named conversion events before any spend goes live. Verify events fire correctly in both platforms' debug tools. Do not launch paid campaigns without verified conversion tracking.
2. Set up UTM parameters on every ad URL — use a consistent naming convention: utm_source / utm_medium / utm_campaign / utm_content. This is the only reliable cross-platform attribution you will have in months 1–3.
3. Launch Google Search tutor campaigns (Campaign 1) in week 1. Parent campaigns (Campaign 2) in week 3 once tutor campaigns have baseline data.
4. Launch Meta tutor prospecting (Interest audience) in week 1. Do not launch Lookalike until month 3.
5. Create the TikTok organic video first. Only boost it as a Spark Ad once it has 500+ organic views — organic validation is the precondition for paid amplification.
6. Build retargeting audiences in Meta from day 1 (even at low traffic, they need 30 days to accumulate before they can be targeted). Do not spend on retargeting until month 2.
7. Set up a simple weekly dashboard in Google Looker Studio (free) pulling from GA4, Google Ads, and Meta via a connector. Avoid manual CSV pulls — they create reporting lag and errors.
