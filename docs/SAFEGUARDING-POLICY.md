# fair-do Safeguarding Policy

**Version 1.0 — July 2026**

---

## 1. Purpose

fair-do is a practice-management platform that enables tutors to teach students, including children and young people, via secure online video. This policy sets out:

- fair-do's commitment to the safety and welfare of children and young people who use the platform;
- the obligations all tutors accept when they sign up; and
- the procedure for reporting and responding to safeguarding concerns.

This policy applies to all tutors registered on fair-do and to fair-do as the platform operator.

---

## 2. Definitions

**Child / young person** — anyone under the age of 18.

**Safeguarding concern** — any situation in which a child may be at risk of abuse, neglect, exploitation, or significant harm, including:
- physical, emotional, or sexual abuse;
- neglect or domestic abuse within the home;
- online exploitation or grooming;
- self-harm or suicidal ideation disclosed by a student; or
- conduct by a tutor that may put a student at risk.

**LADO** — Local Authority Designated Officer. The statutory contact for allegations against adults who work with children in the relevant local authority area.

---

## 3. Tutor obligations

By signing up to fair-do, tutors confirm that they:

1. **Hold a valid enhanced DBS check** (or equivalent), as verified by fair-do during onboarding, and will notify fair-do immediately if their DBS status changes.

2. **Are not on the DBS Children's Barred List** and have never been prohibited from working with children.

3. **Will conduct lessons appropriately** — no recording of sessions without prior written consent from the student (or their parent/guardian if under 18), no inappropriate contact, no sharing of personal contact details with students outside the platform, and no conduct that could reasonably be perceived as grooming.

4. **Will act on any disclosure** — if a student discloses abuse, neglect, or harm to themselves or others, the tutor will:
   - listen calmly and without judgement;
   - not promise confidentiality;
   - make a note of what was said in the student's own words; and
   - report to fair-do within 24 hours using the process in section 5.

5. **Will report concerns about platform content or other users** — if a tutor becomes aware of another user behaving inappropriately, or discovers concerning content (e.g. in a shared file), they will report it to fair-do promptly.

6. **Will not use the platform to contact students outside lesson times** for personal or non-educational purposes.

---

## 4. fair-do's commitments

fair-do will:

1. **Verify tutor credentials** — including enhanced DBS check and teaching qualification — before tutors can teach or accept bookings, and monitor expiry dates with automatic suspension if credentials lapse.

2. **Keep student data secure** — in accordance with the fair-do Privacy Policy and UK GDPR.

3. **Respond to safeguarding concerns promptly** — within 24 hours for all reports, and on the same day for any concern indicating a child may be in immediate danger.

4. **Refer to the appropriate authority** when required — including the DBS, the Teaching Regulation Agency (TRA), the LADO, or the police, without necessarily informing the subject of the referral.

5. **Maintain records** of all safeguarding concerns, actions taken, and outcomes, in accordance with data retention obligations.

6. **Suspend accounts** where there is a credible safeguarding concern, pending investigation.

7. **Refer to the DBS** if a tutor is dismissed or removed from the platform for reasons related to safeguarding, as required by law.

---

## 5. How to report a concern

### As a tutor

If you have a safeguarding concern about a student, another tutor, or any content or behaviour on the platform:

1. **If a child is in immediate danger** — call 999 first. Then notify fair-do.
2. **All other concerns** — email **support@fair-do.com** with the subject line **SAFEGUARDING**. Include:
   - the student's first name and your fair-do username;
   - a factual account of what was said or observed (use the student's own words where possible);
   - the date and time; and
   - any supporting evidence (e.g. a screenshot of a message).

Do not attempt to investigate the concern yourself or alert the person the concern is about.

### As a parent or student

Email **support@fair-do.com** with the subject line **SAFEGUARDING**, or call the NSPCC helpline on **0808 800 5000** (free, 24/7).

## 7. Record keeping

All safeguarding concerns reported to fair-do are recorded securely, regardless of whether a referral to an external agency is made. Records are retained for a minimum of 10 years (or until the child reaches the age of 25, whichever is later), in accordance with best practice guidance.
