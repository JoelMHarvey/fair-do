# Tutor Recruitment Kit — fair-do.com

Internal reference for the founding team. Not for public distribution as-is.

## The one-line pitch

Tutors on fair-do keep **100% of their own students** — 0% commission — and 90% on students fair-do sends them from the marketplace. Set your own rate, your own hours, and we handle all the bookings, video, and payments.

## Why a tutor should care (the numbers)

| | Tutorful | MyTutor | fair-do |
|---|---|---|---|
| **You keep** | ~65–70% (35% service fee) | ~48–52% (40% commission + VAT) | **100% own students · 90% marketplace** |
| **Set your own rate** | Yes | No — banded pricing structure | Yes |
| **Set your own hours** | Yes | Limited by student demand windows | Yes |
| **Payout timing** | Weekly in arrears | Monthly | Per lesson (Stripe, same or next day) |
| **Student lock-in tactics** | Messaging wall; cannot exchange contact details | Platform-mediated only | None — your student relationship, your contact |
| **Subscription to join** | No | No | No |
| **Commission rate** | 35% service fee (student-facing) | ~40% + VAT | **0% own students · 10% marketplace** |

**What that means in practice:** A tutor charging £45/hour with 15 students per week (15 hours) grosses £675/week. On MyTutor they take home roughly £330. On fair-do they keep **the full £675 on their own students** (0% commission), or £607.50 on marketplace-sourced students (10%). Even at the marketplace rate that is £277.50 per week — over £14,000 per year — back in their pocket for identical work.

**Founder note:** We are building the platform tutors deserve — one where a fair rate means the tutor's rate, not ours. The first 15–25 tutors will shape what this becomes, and we want people who care about that. Worth a 15-minute call?

## Who to target (and where)

### 1. Tutorful and MyTutor profile pages (manual identification)

Search Tutorful and MyTutor for tutors in your target subjects and regions. Identify tutors with strong review counts (20+ reviews) who have been on the platform for 2+ years — these are the most frustrated with commission creep. Many include a personal website or LinkedIn in their bio. Reach them there, not through the platform's own messaging system.

### 2. LinkedIn — qualified tutors and teachers

Search: `"private tutor" UK`, `"PGCE" tutor`, `"QTS" tutor`, `"A Level maths tutor"`, `"secondary school teacher" Edinburgh tutor`. Filter to people who describe both teaching and tutoring in their profiles. These are QTS-qualified teachers already supplementing income — the highest-quality cohort. Connection request with a personalised note; do not cold InMail without connecting first.

### 3. Facebook groups

Post in (or message members of) the following:
- **National Tutors UK** — facebook.com/groups/NationalTutorsUK — large national pool, direct recruitment accepted
- **UK Tutoring Network** — facebook.com/groups/1184715999080646 — peer support focus; value-first posts perform best
- **Private Tutors in UK** — facebook.com/groups/559127687630464
- **Twinkl Private Tutors** — Twinkl-affiliated; teacher-adjacent and quality-conscious
- **Ultimate Support Group for Online Tutors** — facebook.com/groups/303391446535402
- Subject-specific teacher groups: "Maths Teachers UK", "English Teachers Network", "Secondary Science Teachers UK" — higher signal-to-noise than generic tutor groups

### 4. TES Community — Private Tutors forum

community.tes.com/forums/private-tutors.105

The professional home of UK teachers discussing private tutoring. Post a clear, non-salesy thread about fair-do's model and invite questions. Teachers on TES are sceptical of marketing but respond to transparent comparisons and honest framing. This is the highest-quality audience for QTS-qualified tutors.

### 5. Reddit

- **r/tutors** — post value-first content about the UK tutoring market, include fair-do as context, not as the headline
- **r/TeachingUK** — teachers discussing professional and financial issues; tutoring income threads get strong engagement
- **r/GCSE** and **r/Alevels** — students and tutors both active; tutors self-promote regularly here
- Be transparent about who you are; Reddit communities flag advertising disguised as organic posting

### 6. Twitter/X — EduTwitter

Use and engage with: `#EduTwitter`, `#MathsTeacher`, `#EnglishTeacher`, `#TeachingAndLearning`, `#UKEd`. Teachers with 5,000–50,000 followers who also tutor are ideal micro-ambassadors. Engage with their content genuinely before pitching. A quote-retweet of a thread complaining about platform fees — with a clear "this is why we built fair-do" response — can generate significant organic reach.

### 7. NQT and ECT networks

Newly Qualified Teachers (now called Early Career Teachers, ECTs) are on low starting salaries (around £30,000–£33,000) and actively looking for supplementary income. Reach them through:
- NQT/ECT Facebook groups (search "NQT 2025 UK", "ECT Secondary 2025")
- PGCE student union pages at Russell Group universities
- LinkedIn targeting PGCE graduates from the last 2 years
- Education department noticeboards at universities (ask permission)

This cohort has no loyalty to existing platforms (they have not been locked in yet), is comfortable with technology, and has high referral potential among their peer group.

### 8. The Tutors' Association

thetutorsassociation.org.uk — the professional body for UK tutors. Attend or sponsor a Regional Event or Community Hub (they run monthly). TTA-affiliated tutors are already DBS-checked, hold references, and work to a code of practice — exactly the founding cohort you want. TTA partnership or membership also functions as a trust signal for parents.

## Objection handling

### "Is it legit / who's behind it?"

**Response:** Fair-do.com is a registered UK company [insert company number when available]. We're based in the UK, founded by [name(s)], and you can find us at [address / Companies House link]. The platform runs on Stripe for payments (meaning your money is handled by one of the most trusted payment processors in the world, not by us directly). We understand that tutors have seen platforms come and go — we are happy to answer specific questions about our structure, team, or backing. The founding cohort gives us an incentive to be here for the long term: we grow when you do.

### "What about my DBS check?"

**Response:** For tutors working with under-18s, an Enhanced DBS certificate is required. You verify and enter your own DBS number during onboarding — we do not handle the DBS process itself, and you are responsible for ensuring your certificate is current (within 3 years is standard; the Tutors' Association recommends within 12 months for active tutors). If you are tutoring adults only, no DBS is required. If you are a QTS-qualified teacher your school DBS will already be on the Update Service — you can use that. If you need a new DBS check, the Gov.uk DBS application route costs £38 for enhanced (correct as of 2025); alternatively the Tutors' Association offers guidance for independent tutors.

### "How many students will I actually get?"

**Response:** Honest answer: we are in early launch and student numbers are growing, not established. We will not promise a number we cannot stand behind. What we can say is: founding tutors receive priority placement in search results, which means you are more visible than any tutor who joins after you. We are investing in SEO and paid acquisition targeting parents actively searching for tutors. We are also launching with a geographic and subject focus rather than claiming to cover everything on day one — this means the students we do have are being matched to a smaller pool. Many tutors join while keeping their existing listings active, building a fair-do student base in parallel without any risk to their current income.

### "What's the catch?"

**Response:** No catch. You keep **100% of your own students** — we charge **0%** commission on those. We take **10% only on students the marketplace sends you**, to cover Stripe processing (around 1.5–2%), hosting, and the marketing that brings you those students. At scale, 10% of a large, growing volume is a sustainable business. Most competitors charge 30–40% because they built margin-extraction into their model from day one. We built ours around tutor retention: if tutors earn more, they stay, they refer others, and the platform grows. The 10% is genuinely the fee — there are no hidden charges, no subscription required to join, and no exclusivity.

### "Do I need to be a qualified teacher?"

**Response:** No. There is no legal requirement to hold QTS or a PGCE to tutor privately in the UK, and we do not require it. Many excellent tutors are graduates, subject specialists, or career-changers. For tutors working with under-18s, a DBS check is required regardless of qualifications. Your profile allows you to show your credentials — degree, PGCE, QTS, subject expertise — and parents can make their own judgements. If you have QTS, display it; it is a strong trust signal. If you do not, your subject knowledge and reviews will speak for themselves.

### "What about my own existing students?"

**Response:** Your existing students are yours. We have no exclusivity clause, no restriction on students you bring to the platform, and no interest in locking you in. You can migrate your existing students to fair-do (they sign up for free and book through the platform) and pay **0% commission** on them — compared to whatever your current platform charges. The 10% applies only to students the marketplace sends you. Or you can keep them off-platform entirely and only use fair-do for new students. You can run both approaches simultaneously. The only constraint: if a student finds you through fair-do's marketplace, lessons with that student should be booked through fair-do.

### "How is the video / tech?"

**Response:** Lessons run in-browser — no download required for you or your student. The video is built into the platform, which means scheduling, lesson, notes, and payment all live in one place. You do not need Zoom, Skype, or any third-party tool (though if you strongly prefer one, nothing stops you using it; the scheduling and payment infrastructure is the part that matters). The in-browser video is optimised for one-to-one lessons and works on any modern laptop or tablet.

### "What if a student doesn't pay?"

**Response:** Payment is collected upfront at booking via Stripe. Students pay before the lesson is confirmed — we do not allow lessons to happen without payment secured. This means you never chase an invoice, never have an awkward conversation about an unpaid lesson, and never lose income to a no-show. If a lesson is cancelled within the cancellation window, the refund policy (set by you within the platform's parameters) determines what the student receives. Late cancellations and no-shows default to protecting your time: the standard setting holds the lesson fee for cancellations under 24 hours.

## Onboarding the tutor — step by step

**Step 1: Sign up and verify email**
Tutor creates an account at fair-do.com/tutors. Email verification required before proceeding. No payment details needed at this stage.

**Step 2: Build your profile**
Enter subjects taught, levels (Primary, KS3, GCSE, A-Level, University, Adult), a short bio, and your headline rate. Upload a profile photo (required — profiles without photos convert at a fraction of the rate). Add credentials: degree, PGCE/QTS status, any relevant qualifications. The profile generates a shareable URL (fair-do.com/tutor/[your-name]) immediately.

**Step 3: DBS verification (if tutoring under-18s)**
Enter your DBS certificate number and issue date. For tutors on the DBS Update Service, the platform performs an online status check. For tutors with a paper certificate, you self-declare and we spot-check via the Update Service where possible. Profiles working with under-18s are not made publicly visible until DBS is confirmed.

**Step 4: Connect Stripe**
Click "Set up payouts" to connect a Stripe Express account. This takes 3–5 minutes and requires your National Insurance number and bank account details. All lesson payments flow through Stripe — your payout hits your bank account within 1–2 business days of each lesson.

**Step 5: Set availability**
Use the availability calendar to set your recurring weekly schedule and any blocked dates. Students can only book slots you have marked available. You can update this any time.

**Step 6: Set lesson rates**
Confirm your hourly rate (or set different rates by level if you prefer). You can offer a free 15-minute introductory call — this is optional but increases booking conversion significantly.

**Step 7: Go live**
Your profile goes live in the marketplace. Share your personal fair-do URL with existing students and on your social channels. Founding tutors are marked with the Founding Tutor badge and placed at the top of relevant search results from day one.

## Founding-tutor incentives to consider

### 1. Priority placement (permanent, not time-limited)

Founding tutors (first 25) receive permanent priority placement in search results for their subjects and levels. This is low cost to offer and has high perceived value — tutors understand that position in a marketplace listing directly affects income. Frame it clearly: "You will never be buried by a later arrival in your subject area." Consider maintaining this for at least 2 years to make the incentive credible.

**Why it works:** Addresses the top objection ("will I get students?") in a way that is free to provide and creates genuine early-mover advantage. Tutors who joined Tutorful in 2016 with 200 reviews are now nearly impossible to displace — founding tutors on fair-do get that same structural advantage.

### 2. Referral scheme — £25 per successful referral

Pay £25 (cash via Stripe, not credit) for each tutor a founding tutor refers who completes their first five lessons. No cap. Founding tutors who refer actively can earn hundreds of pounds within the first few months.

**Why it works:** Tutors talk to other tutors. Word-of-mouth within the TES community, NQT Facebook groups, and EduTwitter is more credible than any marketing you can buy. A founding tutor referring from genuine enthusiasm (because they keep 100% of their own students) is your highest-conversion acquisition channel. £25 per referral is well below the cost of paid acquisition for a tutor who stays.

### 3. Founding Tutor badge (permanent profile marker)

The first 25 tutors receive a visible "Founding Tutor" badge on their profile — permanently. This functions as a social proof signal to parents ("this tutor has been on fair-do from the beginning") and as a status marker within the tutor community.

**Why it works:** Costs nothing. Creates a sense of scarcity and identity. Tutors who feel invested in the success of a platform they helped build are less likely to churn when competitors come knocking. The badge is a commitment device for them as much as a trust signal for parents.

### 4. Direct access to the founding team

Founding tutors get a private WhatsApp or Slack group with the fair-do founders — not a support ticket queue. They can flag bugs, request features, and get answers in hours rather than days. They are consulted before major product changes that affect tutor experience.

**Why it works:** This directly addresses the biggest complaint tutors have about Tutorful ("there is no way to escalate issues and support tickets go into a void"). The cost is a founder's time — which is worth spending on the 25 people most likely to tell every tutor they know whether fair-do is worth joining. This cohort is your advisory board whether you formalise it or not; you may as well treat them like one.

## Track it

### Simple CRM approach

Use a spreadsheet or a free tier of Notion/Airtable. You do not need dedicated CRM software at this stage. Track:

| Column | What to log |
|---|---|
| Name | Tutor's full name |
| Platform (current) | Tutorful / MyTutor / Superprof / Independent |
| Subject(s) | Primary subjects they tutor |
| Level(s) | GCSE / A-Level / Primary / University etc |
| Channel | How you found them (LinkedIn / TES / referral / Facebook) |
| Outreach date | Date of first contact |
| Response | Yes / No / Not yet / Replied with question |
| Status | Interested / Call booked / Signed up / Active / Passed |
| Referred by | If they came via an existing tutor |
| Notes | Anything specific — objections raised, timing constraints |

### Outreach targets

| Stage | Target | Timeframe |
|---|---|---|
| Initial outreach | 100 tutors contacted | Month 1 |
| Responses | 20–30 (20–30% response rate is realistic for personalised outreach) | Month 1 |
| Calls booked | 10–15 | Month 1–2 |
| Signed up (profile created) | 8–12 | Month 2 |
| Active (completed first lesson) | 5–8 | Month 2–3 |
| Founding cohort full | 15–25 active tutors | Month 3 |

### Conversion rates to aim for

- Cold outreach → response: 20–30% (personalised outreach only; spray-and-pray drops to 2–5%)
- Response → call booked: 50–60%
- Call → signed up: 60–70% (the 0%-on-own-students offer does the work once you address the "will I get students?" objection)
- Signed up → active (first lesson): 70–80% (drop-off here is usually profile incompletion or DBS delay — chase these)

### Weekly review rhythm

Each week, ask:
1. How many tutors did we contact? (Target: 20–30/week in early months)
2. How many are now active (completed at least one lesson)?
3. What is the most common objection this week?
4. Which channel is converting best?
5. Are any founding tutors using the referral scheme?

Adjust channel mix based on actual conversion, not assumed quality. LinkedIn outreach to QTS teachers converts differently from Reddit posts — measure both.
